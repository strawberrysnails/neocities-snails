New Website in the Snails Universe

February 28, 2026 | #personal

Table of Contents

  1. # Hello
  2. # Why?
  3. # Widgets
  4. # Please comment
  5. # Bye

# Hello

Well, I've created a new website off-platform! I really want snails.town to be a personal scrapbook, a digital diary, and a home-y place to be. It's time for tutorials and "learning in public" blog posts to move out of the town and into a new space. A blog I'm calling strawberrysnails.

# Why?

I really enjoy writing about coding and making tutorials but it doesn't seem right for my humble town. I also think it would be a good idea to have a bit of a professional or "portfolio" type of site, so that's what this is.

Sometimes I crave a more minimalist approach to web-design, as much as I love making creative and artsy websites, I enjoy very bare-bones blogs too. Finally, I think, this will scratch that itch. I also got to dive into a new framework, Astro! I really like it, especially for minimalist websites.

# Widgets

Strawberrysnails is where all of my coding projects and widgets will live. But I will still link them here, as they are created FOR neocities/nekoweb/personal website users.

Speaking of widgets, I would really love if you would check out my Steam Widgets and let me know what you think. Are they something worth using? Would you prefer a different set up? What other widgets would you use? Hopefully it's within my scope.

# Please comment

I would also love to hear what types of tutorials and code snippets people want to see! I'm now implementing a comment box for each blog post so please share your thoughts and opinions. If you're more comfortable in a private setting you can also email me.

# Bye

Thanks for reading this tiny update! Check out strawberrysnails & subscribe via RSS.
